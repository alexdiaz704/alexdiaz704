I'm Alex Diaz, and I'm a student at the University of California, Riverside right now. In the spring of 2026, I intend to major in political science. I have had the chance to develop greatly while attending UCR, both personally and academically. My understanding of the value of education, discipline, and perseverance has improved as a result of the experiences I've had both inside and outside of the classroom. Over the past few years, my desire to actually change the world has grown and intensified. As I've learned more about how communities, governments, and institutions work, I've been really interested in the systems that shape society and the ways that people can be impacted.

My desire to have a long lasting influence on people's lives is the driving force behind my decision to study political science. Politics is about people, justice, and equality, not just laws or regulations. It offers a technique to deal with the problems that regular people encounter on a daily basis. I've learned that navigating a world that frequently seems daunting or unapproachable requires an awareness of political structures and legal frameworks. Through my education, I hope to acquire the information and analytical abilities needed to speak up for people who lack a voice or find it difficult to be heard.After earning my political science bachelor's degree, I intend to further my education by going to law school or getting a master's degree and finally earning a political science doctorate.

I think that going to college will help me get a deeper grasp of public policy and the legal system, both of which are essential for advancing equality and justice. In addition to satisfying my career and personal goals, these achievements would enable me to make a significant contribution to my community. I want to help people comprehend and make use of the laws that protect them by fusing my love of political science with a commitment to public service. My goal is to enable people to take charge of their rights and enhance their lives, whether through community education, legal work, or advocacy.My quest is ultimately motivated by a strong dedication to compassion, equality, and justice. I'm getting closer to realizing my intense passion to assist people and bring about constructive social change with each academic step I take.
